### Phase 2: Mastering Tension-Free Technique

Tension in the hands and arms is a major barrier to speed, endurance, and quality of tone in piano playing, often causing pain and limiting progress.

| Step | Action Item | Contextual Advice & Source Citation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2.1** | **Avoid Unnecessary Tension** | Adding unnecessary tension into your hands and arms will cause you to tire out more quickly, potentially causing physical pain, and putting a "serious cap on your progress." |
| | | *Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dyhWyOPaOEY (4:19:35 - 4:32:16)* |
| **2.2** | **Practice Hand Relaxation (The "Open Gap" Exercise)** | To actively release tension, practice opening the gap between your thumb and index finger. Note how this specific action causes the forearm to feel "more relaxed." Conversely, closing this gap adds tension. This awareness is key for maintaining loose technique. |
| | | *Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dyhWyOPaOEY (4:32:16 - 4:47:91)* |
| **2.3** | **Prioritize Fundamental Technique** | Recognize that technique is the most important factor enabling you to play effortlessly and without limitations. **Fixing your technique enables you to play faster and longer.** |
| | | *Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dyhWyOPaOEY (5:18:03 - 5:31:28)* |
